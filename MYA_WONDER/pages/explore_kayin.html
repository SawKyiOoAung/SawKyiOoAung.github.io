Kayin State: Nature and Culture United

A blend of nature and culture.

Kayin State, located in southeastern Myanmar, is a land of breathtaking landscapes and rich cultural heritage. Known for its majestic limestone mountains, serene rivers, and lush greenery, Kayin is a paradise for nature lovers and adventurers alike. The region is home to the Kayin (Karen) people, whose vibrant traditions and unique way of life add to its charm. Visitors can explore the stunning caves, picturesque villages, and sacred sites that tell stories of a deep connection between nature and spirituality. Kayin State offers an unforgettable journey into the heart of Myanmarâ€™s natural and cultural wonders.

Origin

Kayin has people from Karen, Padaung, Bamar, Shan, Pa-O, Mon, Rakhine and Burmese-Thai lineages. Most people speak Kayin, Bamar and Mon languages and follow Buddhism, Christianity, Islam, Hinduism and animism.

geography

With a total area of 12,296.6 sq km/ 4,747.7 sq. miles, Kayin is bordered by Thailand, Mon State, Bago Region, Mandalay Region, Shan State and Kayah State and has a hot and humid climate.

interesting facts

Hpa-An is the capital of Kayin State. The state is characterised by mysterious caves, lush green farms and limestone hills. Currently, around 1.57 million (2014) live in Kayin State.
