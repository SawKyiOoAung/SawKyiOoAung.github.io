Kayin State: Nature and Culture United

A blend of nature and culture.

Kayin State, located in southeastern Myanmar, is a land of breathtaking landscapes and rich cultural heritage. Known for its majestic limestone mountains, serene rivers, and lush greenery, Kayin is a paradise for nature lovers and adventurers alike. The region is home to the Kayin (Karen) people, whose vibrant traditions and unique way of life add to its charm. Visitors can explore the stunning caves, picturesque villages, and sacred sites that tell stories of a deep connection between nature and spirituality. Kayin State offers an unforgettable journey into the heart of Myanmar’s natural and cultural wonders.

Origin

Kayin has people from Karen, Padaung, Bamar, Shan, Pa-O, Mon, Rakhine and Burmese-Thai lineages. Most people speak Kayin, Bamar and Mon languages and follow Buddhism, Christianity, Islam, Hinduism and animism.

geography

With a total area of 12,296.6 sq km/ 4,747.7 sq. miles, Kayin is bordered by Thailand, Mon State, Bago Region, Mandalay Region, Shan State and Kayah State and has a hot and humid climate.

interesting facts

Hpa-An is the capital of Kayin State. The state is characterised by mysterious caves, lush green farms and limestone hills. Currently, around 1.57 million (2014) live in Kayin State.

Mandalay's Treasures

Discover Mandalay's treasures through its monasteries, markets, and breathtaking landscapes.

A TRIP DOWN HISTORY

Mandalay, rich in history, takes visitors to the 19th Century as the last royal city before British colonization in 1885. It features the Mandalay Palace, surrounded by a moat, and the Mingun Bell stupa. Notable monasteries and pagodas, including Atumashi Monastery and Maha Mya Muni Pagoda, invite exploration. The Kuthodaw Pagoda holds the world’s largest book, recognized by UNESCO.

RICH CULTURAL HERITAGE

Myanmar is the cultural heart of the country, rich in monuments and art centers. Tourists can see artisans in stone carving and wood carving workshops, as well as bronze casting and silk weaving sites. Zeygo market offers a variety of traditional handicrafts. Mandalay features two 18-hole golf courses and hosts artistic performances, along with delicious local delicacies served by friendly locals.

NEARBY PLACES TO VISIT

The Ayeyarwady River has fostered towns like Mandalay. Nearby Mingun, 10 kilometers away, features the unfinished Mingun Pahtodawgyi stupa. Innwa, 21 kilometers from Mandalay, was a historic capital with notable monasteries and a 1200-meter bridge to Sagaing. Pottery villages like Yandabo and Kyauk Myaung are also nearby.
